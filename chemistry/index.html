4.1 General Properties of Aqueous Solutions

Aqueous Solution - a solution where the solvent is water.

  • Solution - a kind of homogenous mixture
    • Homogenous mixture - a physical combination of 2 or more substances that have one phase â†’ uniform composition
  • Solutions have two parts - the solute and the solvent
    • Solute - the substance that appears in smaller amounts
    • Solvent - the substance that appears in larger amounts
  • Dissolution - the process of a solvent being dissolved into the solvent to form a solution
  • Dissociation - the separation of ions that occurs when a solid ionic compound dissolves
    • This concludes in the separation of charges - cations [+]and anions [-].

Electrolytes

  • An electrolyte is a substance that dissolves in water. When an electrolyte dissolves, its ions also dissociate and make the solution conductable.
    • Many electrolytes are ionic compounds.
  • A non-electrolyte is a substance that does not have its ions dissociate when dissolved in water. As such, it can not conduct electricity
    • This goes to say that non-electrolytes can dissolve in water, but has no ions to form via dissociation. Non-electrolytes dissolve only as molecules.
    • Many non-electrolytes are molecular compounds.
      • Molecular compounds are compounds with no ionic structure, which means they are made up of atoms, not charged ions.

Strong and Weak Electrolytes

  • A strong electrolyte has high solubility in water, which means it readily dissolves and conducts electricity.
    • Note: all ionic compounds that are soluble in water are strong electrolytes. E.g.: NaCl
    • Note: some molecular compounds are strong electrolytes. E.g., HCl [also a strong acid]
    • Strong electrolytes are good conductors.
  • A weak electrolyte has low solubility in water, which means it partially dissolves and conducts electricity for the portion that is dissolved.
    • Note: most weak electrolytes are molecular compounds
    • Weak electrolytes are weak conductors.
    • Note: do not separate weak electrons on the reactant side of reaction!

  • The double arrow is a signifier of chemical equilibrium, where both dissociation and recombination is happening. The processes are thus coined reversible.

Dissociating - Breaking Ionic Bonds and Separating Ions

  • Water is a polar molecule - its partial charges are both positive and negative, so it attracts both cations and anions.
  • When an ionic compound dissolves in water, water pulls on individual ions and solvates them.
    • Solvation is the process where an ion is surrounded by water molecules, arranged in a specific manner to attract the ion.
  • This happens only if the hydration energy is bigger than the lattice energy.

10.7 Kinetic-Molecular Theory

  1. Gases are made of particles; these particles are always in motion. These particles always travel in straight lines unless they hit something, like another particle or its container.
    • This lays out that these particles are subject to macroscopic laws of motion.
  1. The gas is mostly empty space; particles can be regarded as dimensionless points.
  1. Pressure is the act of gas particles imparting some kinetic energy unto the walls of the container.
  1. Assume particles cannot exert any gravitational or magnetic interactions, as well as dipole-dipole interactions.
    • This assumes that ideal gas collisions are completely elastic.
  1. Temperature is proportional to the average kinetic energy in a system.
    • If you increase temperature, particles have more average kinetic energy.
